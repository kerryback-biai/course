The Setup

Your Audience

You are presenting to your executive team. Your breakout group is your board of advisors.

Convince them this is worth funding.

Instructor Feedback

What Makes a Strong Pitch

Evaluation Dimensions

  • Specificity — a concrete use case, not “we’ll use AI for analytics”
  • Business case — who benefits, what decision gets faster, what’s the dollar impact
  • Data readiness — where does the data live, what’s the integration challenge
  • Governance — who validates answers, what are the guardrails
  • Day 1 action — what happens Monday morning, not “we’ll form a committee”

The best pitches are narrow and actionable. “We’ll deploy document AI on our 200 vendor contracts so procurement can answer compliance questions in seconds” beats “We’ll implement an enterprise AI strategy.”

Plenary Presentations

Synthesis: What You Now Know

Plain English = Data Answers

AI agents turn natural language questions into SQL queries, chart generation, and narrative summaries — no technical skills required.

Simple but Not Easy

The technology is simpler than expected (50 lines of code) but production deployment requires governance, validation, and organizational change.

Two Complementary Tools

Database AI for structured data (numbers, trends, KPIs). Document AI for unstructured text (policies, contracts, procedures). Most real questions need both.

The Critical Skill

The human skill that matters most: asking the right questions and verifying the answers. AI amplifies judgment — it doesn’t replace it.

Beyond Data

AI as Thinking Partner

The real power isn’t asking what happened. It’s asking what should we do — and pushing back on the answer.

AI anchors on whatever framing you provide. The human role is challenging assumptions, not outsourcing judgment.

The Thinking Partner Demo

Data Question

“What were Q4 sales for the Energy Division?”

  • One answer. One number.
  • Useful, but limited.

Strategic Question

“I’m considering expanding the Energy Division. What are the top 3 risks based on our data?”

  • Synthesizes across sales, headcount, customer concentration, margin trends
  • Then: “Push back on risk #2 — what’s the evidence?”

The shift from “report pulling” to “strategic dialogue” is where the real ROI lives. But remember: AI anchors on your framing. Challenge its assumptions the way you’d challenge a consultant.

Monday Morning Actions

A. Audit Workflows

Where do people wait for data? Which recurring reports follow the data → analysis → deliverable pattern?

B. Scope a Sprint

Pick one team, one data source, 30 days. Build a prototype and test with real questions from real users.

C. Inventory Documents

Where is knowledge trapped? Policies, contracts, procedures — which collections would benefit from AI-powered Q&A?

D. Establish Governance

Who validates AI output? Who approves deployment? Define this before shadow IT does it for you.

You don’t need a 12-month roadmap. You need one pilot, one owner, one deadline.

The 30-Day Sprint

Week 1

Pick & Prep

Choose one use case. Identify the data source. Get stakeholder buy-in.

Week 2

Build & Test

Connect the data. Build the prototype. Test with real questions from real users.

Week 3

Validate

Compare AI answers to known results. Document accuracy. Identify failure modes.

Week 4

Decide

Go/no-go for expanded pilot. Present findings. Define Phase 2 scope.

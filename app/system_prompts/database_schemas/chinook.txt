## Database: Chinook Music Retailer

A digital music store with 11 tables covering artists, albums, tracks, customers, and sales.

### Tables

**artists** (275 rows): ArtistId, Name
**albums** (347 rows): AlbumId, Title, ArtistId → artists
**tracks** (3,503 rows): TrackId, Name, AlbumId → albums, MediaTypeId → media_types, GenreId → genres, Composer, Milliseconds, Bytes, UnitPrice
**genres** (25 rows): GenreId, Name
**media_types** (5 rows): MediaTypeId, Name
**playlists** (18 rows): PlaylistId, Name
**playlist_track** (8,715 rows): PlaylistId → playlists, TrackId → tracks

**customers** (59 rows): CustomerId, FirstName, LastName, Company, Address, City, State, Country, PostalCode, Phone, Fax, Email, SupportRepId → employees
**employees** (8 rows): EmployeeId, LastName, FirstName, Title, ReportsTo → employees, BirthDate, HireDate, Address, City, State, Country, PostalCode, Phone, Fax, Email
**invoices** (412 rows): InvoiceId, CustomerId → customers, InvoiceDate, BillingAddress, BillingCity, BillingState, BillingCountry, BillingPostalCode, Total
**invoice_items** (2,240 rows): InvoiceLineId, InvoiceId → invoices, TrackId → tracks, UnitPrice, Quantity

### Key relationships
- artists → albums → tracks (one-to-many chain)
- tracks → genres, tracks → media_types
- customers → invoices → invoice_items → tracks
- employees.ReportsTo is a self-referencing hierarchy (org chart)
- playlists ↔ tracks via playlist_track (many-to-many)

### Common query patterns
- Revenue by genre: JOIN invoice_items → tracks → genres
- Customer spending: JOIN customers → invoices
- Top-selling artists: JOIN invoice_items → tracks → albums → artists
- Employee hierarchy: self-join on employees.ReportsTo
